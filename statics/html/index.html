The Bio-Optics and Nano-Photonics Group of IISER-K

The main research area of the group is (i) Nano-optics (ii) Polarization Optics and optical polarimetry and (iii) Biophotonics

Nano-optics

  1. Basic studies on light-matter interactions at the nano scale.
  2. studies on localized surface plasmon resonance in metal nano-structures.
  3. Studies on coupled plasmons, plasmonic Fano resonances.
  4. Spin Orbit interaction of light in micro and nano optical systems.
  5. Weak measurements to amplify spin optical effects and other tiny optical effects.

Polarization optics

  1. Basic studies on polarization (spin)optical effects.
  2. Basic theoretical and experimental studies on polarization (Mueller matrices, Stokes vectors, Jones matrices) and coherence characteristics of scattered light in random medium.

Biophotonics

  1. Develop novel optical spectroscopic approaches to probe sub-diffractional information on the morphology of complex systems such as biological tissues.
  2. Mueller matrix polarimetry and its applications in biophotonics.
  3. Investigate various optical spectroscopic approaches (Elastic scattering, Fluorescence, Raman) for probing biological systems and other complex systems.
